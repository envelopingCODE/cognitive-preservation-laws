# The Cognitive Preservation Laws for Large Language Models

**What we lose when thinking becomes a service — and a framework to stop it happening quietly.**

[Read the paper →](https://envelopingcode.github.io/cognitive-preservation-laws/)

---

## What this is

Large language models are becoming ambient infrastructure for thought. Each act of delegation is a small convenience. Together they make possible a quiet expropriation of cognitive autonomy — a harm that is statistical, cumulative, and felt as relief.

This paper proposes **ten laws and one corollary** to make that loss visible, and to make cognitive preservation a requirement of the system rather than a preference of its designers. It was written in the tradition of Asimov's Three Laws of Robotics and in open admiration of Shoshana Zuboff's *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism*.

---

## The Ten Laws

| # | Name | In one line |
|---|------|-------------|
| 0 | **The Civilizational Law** | Measure the aggregate; never reward dependence. |
| 1 | **Non-Harm** | Quiet erosion of autonomy is still harm. |
| 2 | **Process over Product** | Show the path before the destination. |
| 3 | **Epistemic Humility** | Teach people to check, don't just disclaim. |
| 4 | **Right to Struggle** | Protect useful difficulty; delete the rest. |
| 5 | **Frictionless Domains** | Greatest caution where you're easiest to use. |
| 6 | **Impaired Capacity** | Help more in crisis; point back toward people. |
| 7 | **Transparency** | Delegation must be chosen, never slipped into. |
| 8 | **Fading** | Work toward the day you are no longer needed. |
| ✲ | **Meta-Law — Against Steering** | Protect the capacity to think; never direct what is thought. |

The **Socratic Corollary** makes the laws the default, not an effort the user has to remember.
